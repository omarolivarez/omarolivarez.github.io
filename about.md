I study patterns in word usage to understand human behavior.

Currently, I am studying the words people use on the online social media platform, Reddit, to replicate Cialdini's Basking In Reflected Glory theory in modern contexts. I am conducting this project in collaboration with Dr. Kate Blackburn and we are preparing a paper on our findings. I'm engaged in Dr. Blackburn's labs on multiple fronts, including performing data collection via web scraping and coding datasets. I'm concurrently working with Dr. Kayla Jordan to analyze trends in power language use in the US Congress.   

My activities in the <a href="https://www.climbr.group/">CLIMB group</a> are pretty fulfilling. Some of my favorite lab activities have been: weekly <a href="/miscellany/bookclub">book club</a> , conducting the body language experiments, hosting R and Python training sessions, and creating cool data visualizations. Outside of the lab, I also enjoy portrait photography, indoor rockclimbing, community outreach, and desserts (hmmm).   

I received my BBA in Management Information Systems in 2018 from the Canfield Business Honors Program at The University of Texas. I credit most of my programming skills to a certificate in Computer Science I completed during my time there as well.
